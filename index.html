Our Research

From Manual Inspection to Transparent AI

The Sri Lankan tea industry depends on subjective visual inspection that fails on subtle quality cues. TeaVision replaces guesswork with measurable, explainable AI.

Project Scope

Build a unified web platform that ingests tea leaf imagery from a desktop dropzone, browser camera, or QR-bridged smartphone, then routes it through three specialised AI subsystems with full traceability of decisions.

Research Gap

Off-the-shelf detectors compress 4K phone photos to 640×640px, mathematically erasing the tiny buds and damage spots that determine grade. Existing models also act as black boxes, eroding farmer and factory trust.

Research Problem

How can computer vision and machine learning deliver microscopically-aware and explainable tea quality assessments while making agricultural treatment knowledge instantly searchable in the field?

Main Objective

Deliver an integrated, evidence-driven assessment platform that detects, classifies, and contextualises tea leaf quality with verifiable accuracy, deployable on a standard factory-grade workstation.

Specific Objectives

01

Preserve microscopic features via SAHI-sliced YOLOv8 inference.

02

Replace black-box CNNs with 25 OpenCV-derived, auditable features.

03

Gate inference with an EXIF + variance based environmental QA score.

04

Surface tea agronomy knowledge through a semantic RAG retrieval layer.

Our Solution

Three AI Pipelines, One Unified Dashboard

TeaVision delivers detection, grading, and knowledge retrieval through a clean React frontend backed by a FastAPI service hosting the AI engines.

01

Tea Leaf Detection

YOLOv8s fine-tuned at 1280×1280 over 7 classes — Fresh_Bud_1, Fresh_Bud_2, Old_Leaf, Damaged_Leaf, Damage_Spot, Coarse_pluck, stems — with SAHI slicing for tiny objects and an A–D quality grade derived from class ratios.

  • 148 → 726 augmented training images
  • mAP@0.5 = 32.5% · AdamW, 20 epochs
  • SAHI overlap tiling + NMS merge
02

Grade My Tea

Explainable binary classifier: 25 OpenCV features (11 colour, 3 texture, 7 shape, 4 quality) selected from a 56-candidate pool via ANOVA F-test, then routed through 10 classical models. AdaBoost wins at 96.36% test accuracy with 0 false positives.

  • 5-fold stratified cross-validation
  • Session-based 80/20 split (no leakage)
  • Per-feature contribution breakdown
03

Ceylon Tea Knowledge Base

Hybrid RAG over 156 documents / 473 chunks across 13 tea-domain categories. Sentence-BERT (all-MiniLM-L6-v2, 384-dim) for dense retrieval, BM25 for sparse, and Reciprocal Rank Fusion (α = 0.5) to combine them.

  • MRR = 1.00 · NDCG@5 = 1.00
  • FAISS cosine-similarity index
  • 3-tab UI: Search, Browse, Categories

Bonus — QR Connect: Mobile-to-Desktop Bridge

A FastAPI LAN bridge generates a QR code that opens a lightweight capture page on the user’s phone. Photos taken in the field stream straight into the desktop dropzone — no email, no cables, no workflow friction.

Headline Metrics

YOLOv8s + SAHI Detection

32.5% mAP@0.5 across 7 classes · best F1 = 0.29 at conf 0.109. Per-class AP — Fresh_Bud_2: 0.578, Coarse_pluck: 0.553, Old_Leaf: 0.431.

AdaBoost Grader

96.36% test accuracy on 55 held-out samples · 17/19 high_quality, 36/36 medium_quality · 0 false positives.

Hybrid RAG Retrieval

MRR = 1.00 · P@1 = 1.00 · R@5 = 1.00 · NDCG@5 = 1.00 with FAISS + BM25 + RRF (α=0.5).

Mobile Capture Quality

78.4% image pass rate after 5 quality gates · blur rejections dropped from ~25% to 8% post-deployment.

TF-Lite Leaf Segmentation

94.2% detection rate, 3.1% false positives. HSV fallback: 82.7% / 8.5% on older devices.

Feature Engineering

25 of 56 features selected via SelectKBest (ANOVA F-test). Top drivers: lab_a_mean (0.22), shape_mean_solidity (0.21), lab_b_mean (0.15).
